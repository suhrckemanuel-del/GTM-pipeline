# Value note — akirolabs

**Vertical:** AI / procurement strategy  
**Geo:** EU — Germany  
**Momentum cited:** Series A stage; BDR intern role open; AI-native procurement platform positioning.

## Observation

Procurement strategy AI sits in an awkward spot between two established categories: **spend management tools** (Coupa, Ariba — execution layer) and **strategic sourcing consultants** (McKinsey, PWC — advice layer). Enterprise buyers — CPOs and CFOs — have already bought something in each bucket and default to "what's different about you?" The risk is being mentally filed as "another procurement tool" before the conversation starts. Companies that break through here anchor on a **specific decision** the buyer keeps getting wrong (e.g., "category strategy for indirect spend takes 6 weeks and still lands on gut feel") and show how AI changes that single outcome first — not the whole procurement function.

## Suggestion

Lead with **one strategic decision, one before/after**. Pick the procurement scenario where the status quo is most painful and most visible to a CPO — category strategy refresh, supplier consolidation, should-cost modelling — and build a one-page "without akirolabs / with akirolabs" for that scenario only. Pair with one reference that names the decision type and the outcome (time saved, decision quality improved), not a generic "transformed our procurement" quote. Use that single story for outbound until it converts, then expand.

## Expected impact

Fewer "we already have Coupa" deflections and shorter discovery calls because the buyer walks in already knowing which problem akirolabs solves — not having to figure it out during the demo.
